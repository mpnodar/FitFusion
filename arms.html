Bicep Curls

Equipment needed:

1. Dumbbells: Choose a weight that challenges you but allows you to maintain proper form.
2. Exercise bench (optional): You can perform bicep curls standing or seated on a bench.

Starting Position:

1. Stand up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart.
2. Hold a dumbbell in each hand, arms fully extended down by your sides, palms facing forward.
3. Alternatively, you can sit on a bench with your back straight and feet flat on the floor, holding the dumbbells with your arms extended downward.
4. Keep your shoulders back, chest up, and core engaged throughout the exercise to maintain proper posture.

Execution:

1. Inhale and brace your core. This will help stabilize your spine.
2. Exhale and slowly bend your elbows, curling the dumbbells upward towards your shoulders.
3. Keep your upper arms stationary and elbows close to your body.
4. Focus on contracting your biceps to lift the weights.
5. Continue lifting until your forearms are vertical and the dumbbells are close to, or lightly touching, your shoulders.
6. Hold this position briefly to maximize muscle engagement.
7. Avoid swinging or using momentum to lift the weights.
8. Keep the movement controlled and deliberate.
9 Squeeze your biceps at the top of the movement to maximize muscle contraction.

Repetition and Sets:

1. Aim to perform 8 to 12 repetitions for muscle hypertrophy (growth).
2. If you're aiming for muscular endurance, you can perform higher repetitions.
3. Start with 2-3 sets of bicep curls and gradually increase the number of sets as you progress.

Shoulder Press

Equipment needed:

1. Dumbbells: Choose a weight that challenges you but allows you to maintain proper form.
2. Exercise bench (optional): You can perform shoulder press standing or seated on a bench.

Starting Position:

1. Begin by sitting on a sturdy bench with back support or standing with your feet shoulder-width apart.
2. Hold a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height, palms facing forward, elbows bent, and upper arms parallel to the floor.
3. Your wrists should be straight, and your core engaged for stability.

Execution:

1. Exhale and press the dumbbells upward until your arms are fully extended overhead.
2. Keep your wrists straight and avoid locking out your elbows at the top of the movement.
3. Keep your core engaged and maintain a slight bend in your knees to stabilize your body.
4. Ensure that the dumbbells move in a straight vertical line above your head without leaning forward or backward. This will help target your shoulder muscles effectively.

Repetition and Sets:

1. Aim to perform 8 to 12 repetitions for muscle hypertrophy (growth).
2. Adjust the weight of the dumbbells as needed to challenge your muscles within the target rep range.
3. Start with 2-3 sets of shoulder presses and gradually increase the number of sets as you progress.

Triceps Extension

Starting Position:

1. Stand facing the cable machine with your feet shoulder-width apart and knees slightly bent for stability.
2. Adjust the cable machine's pulley attachment to a high position and attach a straight or rope handle.
3. Grasp the handle with an overhand grip (palms facing down) and step back a few feet to create tension in the cable.

Execution:

1. Position yourself with your torso upright and core engaged.
2. Keep your elbows close to your sides throughout the exercise.
3. Begin with your arms fully extended, elbows tucked in by your sides, and the handle at chest level.
4. Exhale and slowly extend your elbows, pulling the handle downward toward your thighs. Keep your upper arms stationary and your wrists in line with your forearms.
5. Focus on fully contracting your triceps as you lower the handle, squeezing them at the bottom of the movement.

Repetition and Sets:

1. Aim to perform 8 to 12 repetitions for muscle hypertrophy (growth), using a weight that challenges your triceps within the target rep range.
2. Start with 2-3 sets of tricep extensions and gradually increase the number of sets as you progress.
